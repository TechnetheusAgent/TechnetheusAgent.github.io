Vanquisher WR% Trajectory

How to use this chart

Picking what to look at

Hover a line → focuses that character and reveals their Mastery Gap and Meta Skew overlays (see the visual key below).

Click a name in the legend → hides or shows that character.

Hide All / Show All → clears the chart, or brings everyone back. Use this to build a clean side-by-side comparison.

Show Rising / Falling Trends → only shows the characters the forecast says are climbing or sliding the most.

X% Pred. / Obs. Outliers → only shows the characters making the biggest moves. Pred. uses the forecast; Obs. uses recent real history. Change the X% to widen or tighten the cutoff.

Zooming and time travel

Mouse wheel over the chart → zooms into the time window under the cursor (up to 10×). Double-click the chart to reset.

Hover any past day on a line → "rewinds" the forecast: the dashed projection is redrawn as if you were standing on that day, using only the data that existed back then. Lets you see what the model would have predicted at any point in the past.

Forecast controls

Prediction Days → how far ahead to project. 0 shows history only; higher values show the dashed forecast and its uncertainty cone.

Cones ≤ N chars → when N or fewer characters are visible, each gets its own colour-matched uncertainty band. Raise N to see more bands at once.

Switching data source

Bucket → toggles between Vanquisher (the top ~4% of players) and All-rank (everyone). The forecast engine is the same; only the underlying win-rate series changes.

Weighting → toggles how matchups are combined. Even (default) treats every matchup equally — pure character strength assuming you face every opponent the same amount. Pick-rate weighted emphasises the matchups that actually happen often — your expected win rate in the current meta.

What the lines and numbers mean

Visual key

Solid line → historical win rate. Dashed line → forecast. Shaded area → uncertainty range around the forecast. White line (on hover) → Mastery Gap. Black line (on hover) → Meta Skew.

The metrics, in plain English

Tier Score → the character's average matchup win rate. The Weighting picker decides how matchups are combined: Even = simple average across all matchups (pure character strength); Pick-rate weighted = popular matchups count more (expected meta performance). 50% is perfectly balanced; above is favoured, below is unfavoured.

Mastery Gap → how much better top players do with this character than average players do. Positive: rewards skill — gets stronger in better hands. Negative: a "pubstomper" — falls off at high level. Near zero: works the same at any rank.

Meta Skew → whether the character's matchup spread is lopsided. Positive: bullies a few popular opponents (meta bully). Negative: gets countered by a few popular opponents (kryptonite pressure). Near zero: matchup spread is even, regardless of who's popular.

How the forecast works (briefly)

Forecast → blends two forces: momentum (recent direction continues for a while) and equilibrium (extreme scores get pulled back toward 50% over time). It's a short-horizon read — trust shrinks the further out you look.

Uncertainty bounds → a one-standard-deviation range. Grows quickly at first, then levels off — characters can't drift forever, so the cone doesn't either. Wider for volatile characters, narrower for stable ones.
